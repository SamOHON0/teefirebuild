05 / Extinguishers

Fire extinguisher service and maintenance

Supply, install and service fire extinguishers and accessories in accordance with IS 291 and BS 5306.

IS 291 & BS 5306 compliant

TEE Fire Safety systems can supply, install and service fire extinguishers and accessories in your premises in accordance with IS 291 and BS 5306.

Under the Fire Service Act 1981(2003) and the SHWWA General Application Regulations 2007, all persons having control over a premises and/or employers are required to make provisions against the outbreak of fire. One of these requirements is to have an appropriate number of working fire extinguishers installed on your premises and to ensure that they are serviced on a regular basis, in accordance with the Irish Standard IS 291.

Your legal obligation

It is the responsibility of the premises controller or employer to ensure extinguishers are present, appropriate for the risk, and regularly serviced. We handle every part of that for you.

07 / User Responsibilities

What you need to know

Under Irish law, premises controllers and employers have clear obligations when it comes to fire safety. Here is what that means in practice.

The Fire Services Act 1981 (as amended 2003) and the SHWWA General Application Regulations 2007 require all persons having control over a premises, and/or employers, to make provision against the outbreak of fire.

Your responsibilities include ensuring that appropriate fire safety systems are installed, in good working order, and regularly serviced by competent personnel.

Your key obligations

If you control a premises or employ staff, you are responsible for:

  • Installing an appropriate number of working fire extinguishers on the premises
  • Ensuring all fire safety equipment is serviced regularly in accordance with IS 291
  • Maintaining emergency lighting systems to the standards required by IS 3217
  • Keeping fire alarm systems compliant with IS 3218 through scheduled servicing
  • Keeping records of maintenance, tests and any remedial works carried out

Not sure if you are fully compliant? Get in touch and we will review your setup at no cost.

Frequently Asked

Answers to common questions

A quick rundown of the things clients ask most often. Can't find what you are after? Give us a call.

How often should a fire alarm be serviced?

Under IS 3218, fire alarm systems should be serviced on a minimum quarterly basis by a competent person, with a full test conducted annually. We offer contracts that cover all of this, with full documentation supplied after every visit.

Do you service systems you didn't install?

Yes. We regularly take over maintenance of existing systems, regardless of who installed them. We will assess the system on our first visit and flag anything that needs attention.

What areas do you cover?

All 32 counties. Our engineers are based across Cavan, the Midlands, Limerick, Clare and Kerry, with a Dublin office in Saggart. We travel to wherever the job is.

Can you design a new fire alarm system from scratch?

Yes. We design to IS 3218 and BS 5839, and issue a formal design certificate with every new system. Useful for new builds, major refurbishments, or compliance audits.
