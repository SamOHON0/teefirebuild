Case Study

Zero downtime in an 82,000-seat venue

Phased installation sequenced around match days, concerts and tour operations. Full system commissioning across multiple buildings on the Croke Park campus.

The Challenge

Croke Park is more than a stadium. It is a 82,000-seat venue hosting All-Ireland Finals, concerts, events and day-to-day operations across multiple buildings. When the GAA needed fire alarm installation works completed, downtime was not an option.

Any installation had to work around: match day operations, training schedules, hospitality events, tour groups, and the ongoing life of the stadium.

Our Approach

TEE worked with the Croke Park facilities team to plan every phase of the installation around the stadium's calendar. Our engineers worked evenings, off-match weekends and scheduled maintenance windows to ensure there was never an impact on the public-facing operation.

We treated the venue with the respect it deserves. Every piece of cabling run, every detector installed, every bit of testing was done with an awareness that this is a building that matters to a lot of people.

100% quality installation carried out in a live stadium where we never incurred any downtime. His staff were diligent and very helpful. I would have no problem recommending TEE for any fire alarm works.

Stadium Management, Croke Park
