Fire alarm installation and fitout across both Dublin Airport terminals, delivered in a live 24-hour passenger environment with zero operational impact.

Case Study

Ireland's busiest transport hub

Fire alarm detection, control panels, sounders, interfacing and commissioning delivered across T1 extension and T2 new build, on the country's highest-pressure live site.

The Challenge

Dublin Airport handles more than 30 million passengers annually across Terminals 1 and 2. Any fire alarm installation work had to be delivered without disrupting check-in, security, retail or boarding operations. There is no down time at an airport of this scale.

TEE was engaged for fire alarm installation and fitout works across both the Terminal 1 extension and the new-build Terminal 2, covering detection, sounders, control panels, interfacing with building management systems and commissioning.

Our Approach

We planned and sequenced the works around airport operations, delivering installation in tightly-controlled shifts that minimised impact on passenger-facing areas. Our engineers held the necessary airport security clearances and worked alongside the airport's operations team throughout.

Coordination with other trades on an active construction site, a live passenger terminal and a 24-hour security environment required a level of planning and communication that we have built into how we approach every complex project since.

Outcomes

  • Zero passenger impactAll works delivered without disruption to check-in, security or boarding operations.
  • Full certification deliveredSystems handed over fully commissioned, certified and documented to Irish fire industry standards.
  • Complex interface workIntegration with the airport's building management, PA and evacuation systems.

I have found TEE to be professional, diligent and customer-focused at all times to ensure a best-in-class installation and commissioning is achieved.

Damien Garry, Asset Care Manager, Dublin Airport Authority

The Dublin Airport work established TEE's reputation for delivering complex fire safety installations in live environments, a capability that has gone on to underpin much of our work across hospitals, stadiums and industrial sites.
