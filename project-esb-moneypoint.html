ESB Moneypoint

Europe's only fibre laser fire alarm, detection and deluge system, installed across the coal-handling infrastructure at ESB's Moneypoint power station in Co. Clare.

Case Study

A system without precedent in Europe

Fibre-optic detection along conveyors and stockyard, tied into an automated zone-specific deluge response. Designed for the harshest coal-handling environment in Irish energy.

The Challenge

ESB's Moneypoint station in Co. Clare was, for decades, the largest power generation facility in Ireland. The site handles the transport of combustible coal from ship, through a lengthy conveyor system, to the stockyard and onwards to the interceptor. Every metre of that journey carries fire risk from coal dust.

Conventional smoke and heat detection is not always viable across long, open, dust-laden industrial environments. A specialised approach was required.

Our Approach

TEE was selected to install a fibre laser fire alarm, detection and deluge system across the coal-handling infrastructure at Moneypoint. This system uses fibre-optic lines running along the conveyors and stockyard to detect temperature anomalies at the earliest possible stage, triggering a deluge response before any open fire can develop.

At the time of installation, this was the only system of its kind operating in Europe. Delivery required close coordination with the system manufacturer, the ESB engineering team and the conveyor operations team.

Why It Matters

  • Earliest possible detectionFibre laser technology senses heat build-up long before smoke or flame.
  • Full deluge integrationDetection triggers an automated water deluge response at precise zones, limiting damage and downtime.
  • Industrial-grade engineeringEvery component engineered to survive a harsh coal-handling environment over the long term.

Projects like Moneypoint sit at the top end of what TEE delivers. When a client needs technology that does not yet have a precedent in the country, we go and build the capability with them.
