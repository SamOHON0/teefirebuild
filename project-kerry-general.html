Kerry General Hospital

A complete new addressable fire alarm system installed across Kerry General Hospital in Tralee, delivered on time, on budget, in a live 24/7 clinical environment.

Client HSE / Kerry General Hospital
Sector Healthcare
Location Tralee, Co. Kerry
Scope Full System Replacement
Status Completed 2013

Case Study

A full hospital replacement, live

Phased replacement with temporary coverage strategies across wards, theatres and A&E, ensuring fire protection was maintained at every stage of the switchover.

The Challenge

Kerry General Hospital is the main acute hospital serving County Kerry, with wards, theatres, A&E, outpatient services and support facilities spread across a substantial campus. The existing fire alarm system had reached end of life and needed a full replacement.

A fire alarm replacement at this scale, in an occupied hospital, is one of the more challenging installations we take on. Every phase has to be handed over with full coverage maintained at all times. You cannot simply switch one system off and turn the other on.

Our Approach

TEE delivered the project in carefully planned phases, with temporary coverage strategies ensuring that every area of the hospital remained fully protected throughout. New addressable detection, sounders, control panels, interface work and repeater panels were installed zone by zone, commissioned as each phase completed.

We worked alongside the HSE estates team, the clinical departments, and the project management office to coordinate each handover window.

Outcomes

  • Delivered on time, on budgetFull hospital replacement completed to the agreed programme, no surprises on the invoice.
  • Continuous fire coverageHospital remained fully protected throughout every phase of the replacement.
  • Addressable detection throughoutPinpoint device-level alarm and fault identification, making the hospital easier to maintain long term.

Kerry General is one of a number of major healthcare installations we have completed in Ireland. The pattern is always the same: understand the clinical environment, plan the phases, deliver on the programme, and hand over a system the site team is confident using.
