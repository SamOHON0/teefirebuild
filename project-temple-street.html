Case Study

Delivered ward by ward, zone by zone

Addressable detection, sounders and control panels installed across a live children's hospital, with work sequenced around clinical priorities and delivered to IS 3218 standard.

The Challenge

Temple Street is one of Ireland's busiest children's hospitals, treating thousands of young patients every year. When a new addressable fire alarm system was needed across the building, the priority was clear: the installation could not impact patient care, clinical operations or families already under enormous stress.

Children's hospitals are particularly demanding environments. Paediatric wards, ICU areas, theatres, outpatient clinics and support services all run simultaneously. There is never a quiet weekend when everything stops.

Our Approach

TEE worked closely with the Temple Street clinical and facilities teams to plan the installation ward by ward, zone by zone. Work was sequenced to follow the hospital's operational priorities, with night works and weekend shifts used where daytime activity made them impossible.

Noise discipline, dust control, cable management and infection control protocols were treated with the same rigour as the fire system work itself. Everyone on site understood the environment they were working in.

Outcomes

  • Zero clinical disruptionNo impact on patient care, clinical workflows or family-facing areas at any point during installation.
  • Full addressable systemNew system with individual device-level addressability, giving the hospital pinpoint fault and alarm detection.
  • IS 3218 compliantFull commissioning and documentation delivered to Irish fire alarm standards.

The care we bring to hospital work is rooted in the fact that we understand what is at stake. Temple Street is not a commercial building. It is where families go on some of the hardest days of their lives. That shapes every decision we make on site.
